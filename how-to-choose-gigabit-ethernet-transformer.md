# How to Choose the Right Gigabit Ethernet Transformer for Industrial Networking？
*A practical guide to selecting the right Gigabit Ethernet Transformer for industrial switches, PoE cameras, and automotive Ethernet — from Voohu.*
When designing a Gigabit Ethernet Transformer into your industrial networking product, most engineers focus only on insertion loss — but there are five other parameters that matter just as much.
In modern industrial networking, PoE switches, IP cameras, and industrial gateways rely heavily on one small but critical component: the Gigabit Ethernet Transformer.

But not all transformers are created equal.
Choosing the wrong one can lead to signal loss, overheating, or even complete network failure.

So what should you really look for？
Here’s a practical checklist from Voohu (Suzhou Voohu Technology Co., Ltd.).

1. Bandwidth & Return Loss

A true Gigabit Ethernet Transformer must support 1Gbps data rate with low return loss (typically -16dB or better).
Always check the insertion loss curve – it should remain stable up to 100MHz.

2. Common Mode Rejection & EMI Protection

Industrial environments are noisy.
A well-designed transformer reduces common mode noise and improves EMI performance – something many generic brands overlook.

3. PoE Compatibility (if needed)

If your application requires Power over Ethernet, make sure the transformer supports the required current level (e.g. PoE, PoE+, or PoE++). This is where many “Gigabit” transformers fail in real deployment.

4. Operating Temperature Range

For outdoor or factory use, -40°C to +85°C is often necessary.
Cheaper components usually cut corners here.
